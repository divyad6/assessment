Section 1: Orientation

Time Allotted: 10 seconds for each reply

a) What year is this?
(accept exact answer only)

b) What season is this?
(accept either last week of old season or first week of new season)

c) What month is this?
(accept first day of new month or last day of previous month)

d) What is today's date?
(accept previous or next date)

e) What day of the week is this?
(accept exact answer only)

Section 2: Orientation

Time Allotted: 10 seconds for each reply

a) What country are we in?
(accept exact answer only)

b) What province are we in?
(accept exact answer only)

c) What city/town are we in?
(accept exact answer only)

d) What is the street address of this house?
(accept street name and house number or equivalent in rural areas)

e) What room are we in?
(accept exact answer only)

Section 3: Registration

Time Allotted: 20 seconds

Say: "I am going to name three objects. When I am finished, I want you to repeat them. Remember what they are because I am going to ask you to name them again in a few minutes."

(Say the following words slowly at approximately one-second intervals): Ball / Car / Man
For repeated use: Bell, jar, fan; Bill, tar, can; Bull, bar, pan.

Please repeat the three items for me.

Score one point for each word correctly repeated on the first attempt.
If the person did not repeat all three, repeat until they are learned or up to a maximum of five times (but only score first attempt).

Section 4: Attention and Calculation

Time Allotted: 30 seconds

Spell the word WORLD. You may help the person to spell the word correctly.

Say: "Now spell it backwards please."

If the participant cannot spell world even with assistance, score 0.
Write the person's response below the correct response.
Draw lines matching the same letters in the correct response and the response given. These lines MUST NOT cross each other.
The person's score is the maximum number of lines that can be drawn without crossing any.

/5

Section 5: Recall

Time Allotted: 10 seconds

Say: "Now, what were the three objects I asked you to remember?"

Score one point for each correctly remembered object regardless of order.

/3

Section 6: Language (Naming)

Time Allotted: 10 seconds for each item

a) Show the wristwatch or click the button below to show an image of a wristwatch.
Ask: "What is this called?"

A correct response would be “wristwatch” or “watch”. Do not accept “clock” or “time”, etc.

b) Show the pencil or click the button below to show an image of a pencil.
Ask: "What is this called?"

The only correct response would be ”pencil”. Score incorrect for "pen".

Section 7: Repetition

Time Allotted: 10 seconds

Say: "I would like you to repeat a phrase after me:
No ifs, ands, or buts"

The person must repeat the phrase exactly. If they say something like "no ifs or buts," it is incorrect.

Section 8: Reading

Time Allotted: 10 seconds

Get the picture of "CLOSE YOUR EYES" or click the button below to display the image.
Say: "Read these words and then do what it says."

If the participant just reads and does not close eyes, you may repeat: "Read the words on this page and then do what it says" a maximum of three times. Mark this question as correct only if they close their eyes. They do not have to read aloud.

Section 9: Writing

Time Allotted: 30 seconds

Hand the person a pencil and blank piece of paper.
Say: "Write any complete sentence on that piece of paper."

The person must write a complete sentence which makes sense. Ignore spelling errors.

Section 10: Copying

Time Allotted: 1 minute maximum

Give the participant a pencil and eraser and blank piece of paper. Get the image of the intersecting figures or click the button below to display the image.

Say: "Copy this design, please."

Allow multiple tries. Wait until the person is finished and hands it back.

The person must correctly copy the diagram by drawing a four-sided figure between two five-sided figures.

Section 11: Three-Stage Command

Time Allotted: 30 seconds

Ask the participant if they are right or left-handed. Take a piece of paper and hold it up in front of the participant.

Say: "Take this paper in your right/left hand (whichever is non-dominant), fold the paper in half once with both hands, and put the paper down on the floor."

Score one point for each instruction executed correctly.

Instructions for Administration

1. Before the questionnaire is administered, try to get the person to sit down facing you. Assess the person’s ability to hear and understand very simple conversation, e.g., "What is your name?" If the person uses hearing or visual aids, provide these before starting.

2. Introduce yourself and try to get the person’s confidence. Before you begin, get the person’s permission to ask questions, e.g., "Would it be alright to ask you some questions about your memory?" This helps to avoid catastrophic reactions. Then say, "I am going to ask you some questions and give you some problems to solve. Please try to answer as best as you can."

3. Ask each question a maximum of three times. If the subject does not respond, score 0 or Incorrect.

4. If the person answers incorrectly, score 0 or Incorrect. Accept that answer and do not ask the question again, hint, or provide any physical clues such as head shaking, etc.

5. You will need a blank piece of paper, a pencil, an eraser, and a wristwatch. The required images below (click to download) will all be provided when necessary, and images of a pencil and wristwatch are included in case you don't have them on hand. Regardless, please make sure you have something to write with.

Wristwatch Pencil "CLOSE YOUR EYES" Intersecting Figures

6. If the person answers: "What did you say?", do not explain or engage in conversation. Merely repeat the same directions a maximum of three times.

7. If the person interrupts (e.g., "What is this for?"), reply: "I will explain in a few minutes, when we are finished. Now if we could proceed please… we are almost finished."

8. Each section will have a time allotted. Please allow the person a maximum of the time allotted for each question in the section. There will be a timer provided, which you can open using the button on the right of the screen.
